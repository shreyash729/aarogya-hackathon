Why Our NLP Feature is Demonstrated Locally

At Aarogya, our Automated Clinical Documentation feature leverages advanced Natural Language Processing (NLP) to convert spoken clinical notes into text using the Vosk speech-to-text model. This innovative functionality streamlines healthcare workflows by enabling hands-free note-taking.

However, due to technical constraints in our deployment environment, we are showcasing this feature locally rather than in our live demo at aarogya-hackathon.onrender.com. Below, we explain the reasoning and invite you to view our local demonstration.

Technical Explanation

  • Dependency on PyAudio: The Vosk model requires PyAudio, a Python library for audio processing, which depends on the PortAudio C library.
  • Deployment Limitation: Our hosting platform, Render’s free tier, does not support installing system-level libraries like PortAudio, preventing PyAudio from being installed in the live environment.
  • Workaround: In the live demo, we’ve implemented a text input interface for clinical notes to simulate the NLP feature’s output. This allows us to demonstrate the app’s functionality while adhering to platform constraints.

Local Demonstration

We’ve recorded a video showcasing the full NLP feature running locally, where PyAudio and Vosk are installed. The demo illustrates real-time speech-to-text conversion for clinical notes, highlighting the feature’s potential in a healthcare setting.
